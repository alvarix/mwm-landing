Wanllan Mi Wanlle

WMW is a mutual aid group founded to support recent asylum seekers from West Africa. This initiative operates from physical offices and meeting spaces on Flatbush Avenue, Brooklyn and also through a thriving WhatsApp/Telegram group with over 2,300 participants.

About Wanllan Mi Wanlle

"Wanllan Mi Wanlle" (WMW) means "Help Me, I Help You" in Fulani.
The group focuses on helping its members navigate the treacherous waters of our current immigration laws as well as to promote community to overcome isolation and loneliness. All hands are welcome. A large community of allies among native New Yorkers and beyond provide support by donating time, money and wisdom to widen WMW’s impact and sustainability. WMW also benefits from the generous advice from staff at Co-Counsel, NYC and many other organizational partners.

WMW’s support takes many forms, as members and volunteers share their time and expertise. Assistance can take the form of guiding access to public benefits, encompassing Medicaid, food stamps, the NYC ID card, reduced Metro-card fare, and many forms of English language programs. Our volunteers are called “Navigators” and can help members with many of the services listed below, for free, as well as just being friendly listeners and mentors.

The greatest focus in this moment is on the complex asylum process, including reporting to ICE, scheduling remote hearings, and finding accurate legal advice, in general. Members also get help with civil and criminal legal matters and unfair medical bills. Housing and work are also important topics, including employment authorization applications, small business assistance and trading information on apartment shares. Members also join regular soccer games in Prospect Park and monthly collective dinners at the offices.

Community Impact

The resilience and relevance of Wanllan Mi Wanlle are evident in rapid growth. With over 2,300 dedicated members, our community continues to expand, highlighting how members and volunteers can aid each other and the urgency of the needs we address.

Services We Offer

Unfair Medical Bills?

Got a bill from a hospital or doctor which should be paid by your insurance agent or is unexpectedly large? Our volunteer Navigator will help you reduce or eliminate such bills. He has already eliminated bills for thousands of dollars for members over the past month.

Medical Assistance Program

We can provide guidance and support for members to apply for Medicaid benefits. Get assistance to understand eligibility criteria, complete application forms, and address any challenges that may arise during the application process.

IDNYC Assistance Program

Let us help you obtain an IDNYC municipal identification card application and obtain a proof of address letter. We provide information guidance on how to access the service.

ICE Registration Support

Our ICE Registration Support program offers assistance to individuals required to register with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). Obtain guidance on registration requirements and to complete registration forms.

Change of Address Assistance

We can help members update their address with relevant authorities, such as immigration agencies and government offices. Get help with the process for updating address information and assist individuals in completing the necessary paperwork.

Change of Venue Support

Get help in completing a change of venue motion for their immigration court hearings.

Reduced Metro-Card Fare Program

Are you eligible for a reduced-fare Metro-Cards for public transportation? We provide information on eligibility criteria, and assist in completing applications.

Affidavit Review Service

Our Affidavit Review Service offers assistance to individuals in reviewing and preparing affidavits to support their asylum applications. We review affidavits for accuracy, completeness, and compliance with legal requirements.

Work-Permit Assistance

Our Work-Permit Assistance program provides guidance and support for individuals seeking employment authorization. We provide information on eligibility criteria and assist constituents complete their work permit applications.

Legal Consultation & Asylum Application Support

Our Legal Consultation & Asylum Application Support program offers individuals access to legal consultations and assistance with preparing asylum applications, pro se, in partnership with Co-Counsel NYC.

Want to Drive? Learner’s Permit Class and Online Test Preparation

We can help you prepare for the learner’s permit test required for obtaining a driver’s license.

Online Master Calendar Hearing Assistance

Our Online Master Calendar Hearing Assistance program offers support to individuals scheduled for immigration court hearings. We provide access to a computer in our office, guidance on preparing for master calendar hearings conducted online, and assistance in understanding hearing procedures.
